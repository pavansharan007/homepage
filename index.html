Demat Account FAQs

1. What is a Demat Account?

A Demat (short for Dematerialised) is an electronic account used to hold and manage investments like Stocks, Bonds, Mutual Funds in a digital format. Just as you need to have a bank account to deposit your money and earn interest, you need to open a Demat account to buy or sell financial securities such as Stocks, Mutual Funds, Equity Traded Funds (ETFs), SGBs and more. When you buy stocks or shares, they will get credited to your account. When you sell the stocks or shares you have purchased, they will get debited from your account. By opening a Demat account, you do not need to transfer your share certificates physically to the buyer. You need to link your bank account with your Demat account, through which you can easily buy and sell shares. It does not only save your time, but it also reduces the transaction cost.

2. Demat Account vs Trading Account

A Demat account stores your shares; a Trading account is used to buy/sell them. You need both for stock investing.

Key terms include:
  • DP: Depository Participant
  • NSDL/CDSL: Central depositories in India
  • ISIN: Unique stock identification number
  • Beneficiary ID: Your account identification number

4. Is a PAN Card Necessary?

Yes, a PAN card is mandatory for opening a Demat account in India. It is used to verify your identity and track your stock market transactions for taxation purposes.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. What is the stock market?+
The stock market is a marketplace where investors can buy and sell shares of publicly listed companies. It acts as a platform that facilitates the exchange of ownership in businesses, allowing individuals and institutions to invest in companies and participate in their financial growth. When you buy a stock, you become a part-owner or shareholder of that company and are entitled to a portion of its profits, usually in the form of dividends or capital gains if the stock price rises. The stock market plays a crucial role in the economy by helping businesses raise capital to grow and expand through the issuance of shares via initial public offerings (IPOs). It also offers investors an opportunity to grow their wealth over time. Prices in the stock market are influenced by various factors such as company performance, economic indicators, global events, and investor sentiment. While investing in the stock market can be rewarding, it also involves risks, which is why it's important for investors to research, diversify their investments, and make informed decisions.

4. How does the stock market work?+
The stock market works as a platform where buyers and sellers come together to trade shares of publicly listed companies. When a company wants to raise money, it can sell parts of its ownership in the form of shares through an Initial Public Offering (IPO). Once these shares are available on the stock market, anyone can buy or sell them. This buying and selling happens through stock exchanges like the NSE (National Stock Exchange) or BSE (Bombay Stock Exchange) in India. The prices of stocks change constantly based on supply and demand. If more people want to buy a stock, the price goes up. If more people want to sell, the price goes down. These price changes are also influenced by news, company performance, economic conditions, interest rates, and global events. Investors usually use brokers or online trading platforms to place orders to buy or sell. Once an order is matched on the exchange, the transaction is completed, and the buyer gets ownership of the shares, which are stored in their demat (dematerialized) account. This entire process is managed electronically and takes just a few seconds.
